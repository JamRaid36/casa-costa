# Casa Costa — Connoisseur Content Reference
*From Sonnet research agent, 2026-07-04. Source of authentic detail for copy. Accuracy matters — real aficionados read this.*

## The soul of the site: El Lector (Heritage anchor)
Ybor City rollers collectively hired and PAID a **lector** — a reader who sat on an elevated platform ("la tribuna") in the middle of the factory floor and read aloud all day: newspapers, political tracts, serialized novels the workers voted on. It was informal education for an immigrant workforce; lectors held real cultural authority. Banned in 1931 after a strike (owners saw them as labor-organizing hubs); radio replaced them. THIS is the emotional center — the idea that a cigar house is a place of gathering, story, and voice, not just smoke. Brand voice can echo it ("Pull up a chair. Someone always has a story.").

## Ybor / Cigar City (Heritage facts, all real)
Founded 1880s by Vicente Martínez Ybor, moved from Key West/Cuba to Tampa. Cuban/Spanish/Italian immigrant rollers. 1920s peak: 200+ factories, ~12,000 workers, ~500M hand-rolled cigars/year → Tampa = "Cigar City" / "Cigar Capital of the World." Genuine American immigrant-craft history, not marketing gloss.

## The Reserve (connoisseur heart — feature these accurately)
- **Vitola** = correct term for size/shape (never "type"). Parejo (straight) vs Figurado (tapered). Ring gauge = diameter in 64ths inch; fatter = slower/cooler/more complex, thinner = wrapper-forward/faster.
- Feature vitolas: **Corona** (5.5", 42-44 — the classic benchmark), **Robusto** (5", 50-54), **Toro** (6", most popular in US), **Churchill** (7", named for Winston), **Lancero** (7"+, 38-40 — the purest test of a blend, a master roller's showcase), **Torpedo/Belicoso** (tapered head — note: brands use these interchangeably, no hard standard).
- **Wrappers** (feature 3-4): Connecticut Shade (mild — creamy, nutty, cedar), Maduro (a PROCESS not a plant — extended fermentation → cocoa, coffee, molasses; "dark ≠ strong; strength comes from the filler underneath" is the connoisseur-correcting line), Habano (spicy — leather, earth, pepper), Corojo (peppery kick), Connecticut Broadleaf (dark chocolate, raisin, licorice).
- **Regions to name**: Cuba's Vuelta Abajo (the benchmark); Nicaragua's Estelí (volcanic soil → full/spicy), Jalapa (red clay → smooth/creamy/sweet), Condega (cinnamon, medium). Ecuador (cloud-cover-grown wrapper). Dominican Cibao Valley.
- **Puro** = all tobacco from one country (e.g., "Nicaraguan puro").
- Construction: long-filler hand-rolled (torcedor = roller) is table stakes. Well-made = good **draw** (like a milkshake through a straw — firm not tight), even **burn**, firm light-gray **ash**.

## Humidor / Lockers (Membership angle)
Spanish cedar lining (aromatic oils season the leaf, repels insects, buffers humidity). Target **69-72% RH** (headline number). **Plume/bloom** = flat dry crystalline oils on a well-aged cigar = GOOD, wipes off (never confuse with fuzzy raised mold — that's the amateur tell). **Sick period** = first 3-12 months, ammonia from fermentation, harsh — serious houses rest inventory past it (like wine). **Personal member lockers** = private climate-controlled drawer under your name in the house humidor. Real status feature — use for Membership.

## The Ritual (a section or woven detail)
Cut clean (guillotine / punch / V-cut), never bite. Light with butane or a **cedar spill** — NEVER a petroleum lighter (fluid taints the foot). **Toast the foot** first (angle above flame, rotate) then draw. Don't inhale — the palate does the work. **Retrohale** (exhale a little through the nose) reveals notes the tongue misses — mark of a practiced palate. ~one puff a minute. The **nub** (last inch) is where a great cigar's flavors concentrate.

## Pairings (section)
Logic = match BODY/intensity, not flavor. **Bourbon** = the universal default (vanilla/caramel/oak). **Rye** = spicier, pairs with peppery Corojo/Habano. **Islay Scotch** (Laphroaig/Lagavulin — peaty) = only for full earthy cigars. **Aged rum** = versatile, echoes maduro sweetness, Caribbean-cigar heritage. **Cognac** = elegant, medium cigars. **Espresso** = morning Connecticut-wrapped. **Port** = after-dinner with maduro/oscuro.

## Brands to name-drop for credibility (sparingly)
Cuban: Cohiba, Montecristo, Partagás. New World: Padrón (box-pressed Nicaraguan puros), Arturo Fuente / OpusX, Davidoff, Oliva (founded Tampa 1934!), My Father, Drew Estate. Note: **Oliva's Tampa roots** tie straight into the Ybor/Florida heritage angle.
